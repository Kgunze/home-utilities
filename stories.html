Chapter 1

Origins

1992-1999

A child is born, starts at Kindergarten #25 in District 5, and then faces a broken early school path shaped by family conflict before finally landing at Phan Van Tri Primary School.

A

This is the beginning of the book: home as weather, family as architecture, and the small repeated scenes that later become the logic behind memory.

New storyline

I later realized my aunties were often the ones truly there for me. Their care helped provide a roof over my head and much of the material support that should have made student life stable, but it still was not enough to calm the unstable mindset forming inside me.

That was the beginning of a contradiction I would carry for years: being provided for on the outside while feeling unsafe in my own mind. Looking back, it feels like one of the earliest roots of the PTSD and ADHD that would become clearer later in life.

Chapter 2

School Years

2000-2008

A chapter about routine, classrooms, family expectations, and the difference between what looked normal from the outside and what it felt like internally.

  • Daily structure begins shaping identity
  • Achievement and pressure start to overlap
  • Private emotions stay harder to name

Memory fracture

During primary school, I started losing my sense of time. Looking back, that feeling reaches across kindergarten, primary school, secondary school, and high school as one long period of fragmented memory.

Much of it feels like blank space, without a clear beginning or ending. What remains is often only scattered images and pressure rather than a stable timeline I can fully trust.

Chapter 3

Identity and Pressure

2009-2014

This chapter is about adolescence and early adulthood, when private stress becomes more visible but not always more understandable to the people nearby.

The tone here shifts from observation to interpretation. It is the chapter where conflict, expectation, self-image, and survival strategies begin to harden into patterns.

Escaping the mind

This was the period when I started questioning my existence in this life and in this family. I hid in internet cafes for as long as possible, using games to numb a racing mind rather than to build friendships or learn.

What looked like distance from the outside was often an attempt to survive the intensity of what was happening inside. Many of those lost connections only became possible to recover much later during my working years.

Chapter 4

Work, Home, and Fracture

2015-2020

The fourth chapter follows adult responsibility, household strain, changing roles, and the kinds of breakdown that are often misunderstood while they are still unfolding.

Some years are not remembered as events. They are remembered as pressure.

Chapter 5

Treatment and Reframing

2021-2023

This chapter marks the move from unnamed distress toward records, treatment, psychiatric language, and the effort to separate symptoms from blame.

  • Symptoms become documented instead of guessed
  • Medical language creates a new frame
  • Care, follow-up, and interpretation begin to matter more than silence

Chapter 6

Rebuilding Systems

2024-2026

The current chapter focuses on rebuilding daily life through systems: food, shopping, planning, documentation, design, and a more intentional definition of home.

The book is still open here. The present is not the conclusion; it is the section where structure becomes part of healing and storytelling becomes part of order.

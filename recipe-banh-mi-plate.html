Crisp morning banh mi plate for easy hosting.

This is less a strict recipe than a generous breakfast arrangement built from crisp bread, eggs, herbs, pickles, and whatever fresh things the morning gives you.

Read the method Back to recipe shelf

Story

Breakfast as a small invitation.

A banh mi plate works when you want breakfast to feel thoughtful without becoming complicated.

Toasted bread brings the structure, but the real pleasure is in the contrast: creamy eggs, cool cucumber, sharp pickles, herbs, and a little chili if the table wants it.

It is especially useful for mornings when guests drift into the kitchen at different times. Everything can be arranged ahead, then refreshed quickly before serving.

Method

Assemble more than you cook.

  1. Toast or warm the banh mi until crisp outside and soft inside.
  2. Prepare eggs the way you like, then arrange herbs, cucumber, pickles, and sauces on a board.
  3. Serve everything family-style so each person can build their own breakfast plate.

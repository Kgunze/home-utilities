Recipes / Vietnamese Cooking

Caramelized thit kho for the rice-centered table.

Pork, coconut water, and eggs come together in a glossy braise that feels steady, generous, and deeply familiar.

Read the method Back to recipe shelf

Story

A family dish that rewards low heat.

Thit kho tastes like the kind of meal that was never meant for just one person.

The sweetness here is not sugary. It is slow, dark, and balanced by fish sauce and black pepper. Each piece of pork takes on the broth until it feels almost polished.

Served with hot rice and boiled greens, this is the kind of dinner that anchors the whole table. It holds up beautifully for the next day too, which makes it even more practical for home cooking.

Method

Braise until the sauce clings.

  1. Caramelize sugar carefully, then add pork belly or shoulder and coat it in the amber base.
  2. Add coconut water, fish sauce, shallot, and boiled eggs, then simmer low until the pork turns tender.
  3. Finish with black pepper and serve with steamed rice and a sharp vegetable side.
